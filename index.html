About

I am an Assistant Professor of Data Science and Computer Science at Saint Mary’s College of California. My work brings together responsible artificial intelligence, human–AI interaction, Bayesian modeling, cognitive and computational modeling, and data science education.

My research asks how people interpret, trust, and use AI-generated information, especially when that information may contain bias, uncertainty, or social consequences. I am particularly interested in building transparent, human-centered AI systems that support better judgment rather than replacing human thinking.

Research

LLM Bias and Transparency

We study how bias appears in large language model outputs and how users respond when bias information is displayed at the point of interaction. This work connects responsible AI, interface design, behavioral experiments, and mixed-effects statistical modeling.

Bayesian Bias Aggregation

We develop Bayesian frameworks for combining bias ratings from multiple AI systems and human evaluators. The goal is to move beyond a single bias score and instead quantify both estimated bias and uncertainty.

Human–AI Trust and Decision-Making

Our work examines when transparency helps users make better judgments, when it may increase over-reliance, and how interface cues shape trust, confidence, and intended use of AI-generated content.

Multi-Agent Negotiation Modeling

This research explores computational models of negotiation, interaction, and consensus among multiple agents. The work is connected to decision-making, personality, trust, and human-like behavior in complex systems.

Astrocyte-Inspired Neural Networks

We investigate how ideas inspired by astrocytes and brain dynamics can inform new neural network architectures, including slower modulatory processes, stabilization mechanisms, and biologically inspired learning.

AI in Data Science Education

We design and evaluate AI-supported learning tools for statistics, programming, and data analytics, with a focus on responsible use, student agency, and meaningful learning rather than simple answer generation.

Join the Lab
