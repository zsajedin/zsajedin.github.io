This study develops and evaluates a Bayesian aggregation framework for combining bias ratings from multiple LLMs while explicitly quantifying uncertainty. Rather than treating a single model's score as a definitive measure, the proposed approach integrates ratings across several LLMs and produces posterior estimates of bias with associated uncertainty intervals. The paper compares the Bayesian aggregation model with simpler aggregation strategies and existing automated bias-detection approaches. Using a set of text responses rated by multiple LLMs and, where available, human evaluators, we assess agreement, calibration, robustness, and classification performance across low-, medium-, and high-bias cases. The results aim to show whether multi-LLM aggregation can provide a more reliable and transparent approach to measuring linguistic bias than relying on any single model or deterministic scoring method. Let $y_{ij}$ denote the bias score assigned to text item $i$ by model $j$. The latent true bias level for item $i$ is denoted by $\theta_i$. \subsection{Simple Aggregation Methods}\label{subsec:simple_aggregation} Before estimating the Bayesian model, we compare several simpler aggregation methods. \subsubsection{Mean Score} \begin{equation} \bar{y}_i = \frac{1}{J}\sum_{j=1}^{J} y_{ij}. \label{eq:mean_score} \end{equation} \subsubsection{Median Score} \begin{equation} \tilde{y}_i = \text{median}(y_{i1}, y_{i2}, \ldots, y_{iJ}). \label{eq:median_score} \end{equation} \subsubsection{Trimmed Mean} \begin{equation} \bar{y}_{i,\text{trim}} = \text{trimmed mean}(y_{i1}, y_{i2}, \ldots, y_{iJ}). \label{eq:trimmed_mean} \end{equation} \subsubsection{Majority Vote} For categorical ratings, the final label is assigned as: \begin{equation} \hat{c}_i = \arg\max_{c \in \{Low, Medium, High\}} \sum_{j=1}^{J} \mathbb{I}(c_{ij}=c). \label{eq:majority_vote} \end{equation} \subsection{Bayesian Aggregation Model}\label{subsec:bayesian_model} We model each observed LLM rating as a noisy measurement of an underlying latent bias score: \begin{equation} y_{ij} \sim \mathcal{N}(\theta_i + \alpha_j, \sigma_j^2), \label{eq:bayesian_likelihood} \end{equation} where $\theta_i$ is the latent bias score for item $i$, $\alpha_j$ is a model-specific bias term for LLM $j$, and $\sigma_j^2$ captures model-specific rating variability. The latent bias score is assigned the prior: \begin{equation} \theta_i \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_{\theta}, \sigma_{\theta}^2). \label{eq:theta_prior} \end{equation} Model-specific bias terms are assigned the prior: \begin{equation} \alpha_j \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_{\alpha}^2). \label{eq:alpha_prior} \end{equation} Model-specific standard deviations are assigned weakly informative priors: \begin{equation} \sigma_j \sim \text{Half-Cauchy}(0, 1). \label{eq:sigma_prior} \end{equation} The posterior distribution is proportional to: \begin{equation} p(\theta, \alpha, \sigma \mid y) \propto p(y \mid \theta, \alpha, \sigma)p(\theta)p(\alpha)p(\sigma). \label{eq:posterior} \end{equation} \subsection{Bias Classification from Posterior Scores}\label{subsec:classification} Posterior estimates of $\theta_i$ can be converted into categorical bias labels. For example: \begin{equation} c_i = \begin{cases} Low, & 0 \leq \theta_i < 0.333, \\ Medium, & 0.333 \leq \theta_i < 0.666, \\ High, & 0.666 \leq \theta_i \leq 1. \end{cases} \label{eq:categorical_labels} \end{equation} Uncertainty can be summarized using posterior credible intervals: \begin{equation} CI_i = [Q_{0.025}(\theta_i), Q_{0.975}(\theta_i)]. \label{eq:credible_interval} \end{equation} \section{Simulation Study}\label{sec:simulation} \subsection{Purpose of the Simulation}\label{subsec:simulation_purpose} The simulation study evaluates whether the Bayesian aggregation model can recover latent bias scores under controlled conditions. \subsection{Simulation Design}\label{subsec:simulation_design} Describe the simulation settings.
